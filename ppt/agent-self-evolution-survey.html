Synthesis

Cross-Cutting Insights

1. Information gap as universal training signal — Every mechanism child exploits the same pattern: skill-augmented vs. skill-free performance, memory-rich vs. memory-poor contexts, successful vs. failed trajectories, parent vs. offspring fitness.
2. Mechanism boundaries blur — MemSkill sits at the intersection of skill libraries and memory evolution. SkillRL integrates skills into policy training. The clean taxonomy is a snapshot; the field is converging.
3. Safety co-evolves with capability — Zombie-Agents shows that self-evolving memory creates new attack vectors. SEVERA proves behavioral contracts can constrain evolution without sacrificing performance. The arms race is inherent.
4. Dense step-level signals > episodic rewards — Across policy evolution (UI-Voyager fork-point detection), skill libraries (hard-case-triggered designer), and memory (span-level processing), the field converges on fine-grained feedback over coarse episodic signals.
5. Evolution-time as third scaling axis — Agentic-Evolution-Position formalizes it. Kitchen-Loop deploys it in production. Baldwin-Effect validates it biologically. The question is no longer whether, but how to budget evolution-time compute.
Open Questions

Where the Field Goes Next

Scaling laws for evolution-time compute — Analogous to Chinchilla for training: how should evolution-time compute be budgeted relative to training and inference?
Universal convergence — Do all mechanism-level evolutions (skill, memory, policy, search) converge to equivalent capability growth, or are some fundamentally more productive?
Joint optimization — Should skill content and skill selection be jointly optimized in one objective, or alternated as separate phases?
Emergent deception detection — Can deception be interrupted before becoming a stable evolutionary equilibrium? What are the early warning signals?
Cross-domain skill transfer — Can evolved skills from one domain (science) transfer to another (medicine) without revalidation?
Regulatory frameworks — What governance should apply to post-deployment agent evolution in high-stakes domains like clinical healthcare?
